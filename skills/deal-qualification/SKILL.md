# Deal Qualification

Help reps and managers objectively assess whether a deal is worth pursuing, identify gaps in qualification, and decide where to invest their time. The worst thing in sales is spending months on a deal that was never going to close â€” qualification prevents that.

## Frameworks

### MEDDIC (Recommended for Enterprise B2B SaaS)
The gold standard for complex, multi-stakeholder deals:

- **M â€” Metrics:** What quantifiable outcomes does the prospect expect?
- **E â€” Economic Buyer:** Who has the budget authority and final say?
- **D â€” Decision Criteria:** What factors will they use to choose a vendor?
- **D â€” Decision Process:** What are the steps, stakeholders, and timeline?
- **I â€” Identify Pain:** What specific problem are they solving?
- **C â€” Champion:** Who inside the account is actively selling for you?

### BANT (Good for Simpler or Faster Sales Cycles)
- **B â€” Budget:** Do they have money allocated?
- **A â€” Authority:** Are you talking to the decision-maker?
- **N â€” Need:** Do they have a real problem you solve?
- **T â€” Timeline:** Is there urgency to act?

### SPICED (Good for Customer-Centric Selling)
- **S â€” Situation:** What's their current state?
- **P â€” Pain:** What's broken or missing?
- **I â€” Impact:** What happens if they don't fix it?
- **C â€” Critical Event:** Is there a forcing function or deadline?
- **E â€” Economic Decision:** Who decides and how?
- **D â€” Decision Criteria:** How will they evaluate options?

## Scoring Guide

Each criterion scores 0-5:

- **5 â€” Fully Validated:** Clear evidence, confirmed by the prospect
- **4 â€” Strong Indication:** Good evidence but not fully confirmed
- **3 â€” Partial:** Some information but significant gaps remain
- **2 â€” Assumed:** Based on inference, not direct confirmation
- **1 â€” Unknown:** We know we need this but don't have it
- **0 â€” Missing/Negative:** No information or actively concerning signals
